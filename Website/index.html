ABOUT DATA

Airline, as the most efficient and convenient traffic method, has drawn much public attentions. With an increasing number of persons choose airline as their preferences, more and more concerns have been cast to how long it takes, how safe it is, and of course, how much it costs. In particular, most of us must have been confused about the flight price, which seems to be influenced by lots of causes. Today, we take the great of the method of data analysis to visualize the underlying relationships among some considerable causes.

Finding1

Calendar view

After simply casting the monthly number of airline passengers and the diesel price to the calendar, we can easily acquire the vision impression of the numbers. In the figure above, every single cell stands for a day, and the color of the cell reflects how big the number is. In particular, the darker the color shows, the bigger the number is..

Finding2

Trend

Knowing the bubble size and number labeled on it responds to the passenger numbers, to our surprise, we can see that while the diesels are in low prices, the passenger numbers are actually in the decline, which is far from our expectation that low prices reflect low flight tickets and more passengers. Consequently, diesel prices are decided mostly by requirement.

Finding3

Automation

( X-axis stands for time range. Y-axis is the according value at certain time.) There are three aspects we need to pay attention from our charts. Firstly, the relationship between diesel prices and fuel consumption. Often people presume travel agencies have less airlines routine when diesel prices are high, thus the fuel consumption becomes less. Secondly, the relationship between the number of passengers and diesel price, by which we can found out if airline passengers are on the rise when diesel is in low price, or on the contrary.
